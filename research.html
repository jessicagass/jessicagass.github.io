Research

A research program centered on employee well-being and everyday work experiences.

This work examines how stress, commuting, affect, and meaningful non-work experiences influence recovery, safety, and performance across the employee lifecycle.

Overview

Research agenda

Jessica's scholarship investigates how workers navigate demands across work and life domains, with special attention to stress recovery, emotional processes, and occupational health.

Across projects, the aim is to produce evidence that is theoretically grounded, methodologically rigorous, and useful for organizations seeking to foster healthier and safer work environments.

Current Projects

Selected lines of inquiry

Workplace Experiences and Identity

Investigating how workplace stigma and identity-related experiences shape sleep, stress, and well-being outcomes.

Commuting, Affect, and Recovery

Examining how commuting experiences influence emotions, rumination, safety-related events, and subsequent job performance.

Volunteering and Engagement

Studying how congruence between volunteer motivation and experience contributes to work engagement and recovery.
