Research

My research examines how daily experiences shape employee well-being, recovery, and performance, with particular attention to stress processes across work and non-work domains.

Research Program

My research program focuses on the intersection of work and non-work experiences, with an emphasis on how employees recover from stress and how daily contextual factors influence affective and behavioral outcomes. I draw on experience sampling and daily diary methodologies to capture within-person dynamics across time.

Research Areas

Work Stress and Recovery

Investigating how employees recover from work-related stressors and how recovery processes influence next-day well-being and performance.

Commuting and Daily Dynamics

Examining how commuting experiences contribute to affective states, rumination, and safety-related outcomes in the transition between home and work.

Volunteering and Role Congruence

Exploring how alignment between volunteer motivations and experiences relates to work engagement and recovery processes.

Identity and Workplace Experiences

Studying how identity-related factors, including stigma, shape daily experiences, sleep, and well-being among underrepresented employee populations.
