About Me

I am a graduate researcher at Virginia Tech specializing in industrial-organizational psychology. My work focuses on understanding how employees recover from stress, how commuting and daily experiences influence well-being, and how non-work activities like volunteering shape engagement at work.

Research Highlights

A few of the themes that anchor my current research program.

Work Stress & Recovery

Examining how employees recover from daily stressors and how this impacts performance and well-being.

Commuting & Daily Experiences

Exploring how commuting and morning experiences influence safety, affect, and work outcomes.

Volunteering & Work Engagement

Understanding how alignment between motivations and volunteer experiences affects engagement at work.
