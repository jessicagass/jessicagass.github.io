About

Academic background and research agenda

Jessica studies how employees manage demands across work and non-work domains, with particular interest in recovery, affective processes, and well-being. Her work integrates theory and applied research to better understand how everyday experiences accumulate into meaningful outcomes for workers and organizations.

Her broader agenda emphasizes rigorous, practice-relevant scholarship that can inform healthier workplaces, improve safety, and support sustainable employee performance over time.

Research Themes

Core areas of inquiry

Stress and Recovery

Examining how employees recover from daily stressors and how recovery shapes well-being, energy, and performance.

Commuting and Daily Experience

Exploring how commuting conditions, emotions, and morning experiences influence safety and work outcomes.

Meaningful Non-Work Activity

Studying how volunteering and other non-work roles contribute to engagement, identity, and resource renewal.
